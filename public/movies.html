Jeff Goldblum's Best Movies

A career spanning 50 years. These are the highlights.

#1 1993

Jurassic Park

Directed by Steven Spielberg

The film that made Jeff Goldblum a household name. His Dr. Ian Malcolm — leather-jacketed, chaos-obsessed, perpetually correct — is one of cinema's great intellectual heroes. The shirtless scene. The monologues. The laugh. All of it. This film is the reason this Wordle game exists.

#2 1986

The Fly

Directed by David Cronenberg

Goldblum's greatest dramatic performance. A romantic, tragic, horrifying film about transformation, illness, and love. He was robbed of an Oscar nomination. The Academy has never fully recovered from this oversight. Watch it if you haven't. Then watch it again.

#3 2017

Thor: Ragnarok

Directed by Taika Waititi

The Grandmaster: a role so perfectly suited to Goldblum's energy that it feels like Waititi wrote it by asking "what if Jeff Goldblum but also all-powerful?" The improv was legendary. The deleted scenes are even better. The internet was and remains grateful.

#4 1996

Independence Day

Directed by Roland Emmerich

He saved the world using a laptop and a computer virus. Implausible? Yes. Did it work? Also yes. David Levinson is the everyman genius archetype perfected — a man who brings chess to an alien invasion and still somehow has the solution everyone needs. Of course he does.

#5 2014

The Grand Budapest Hotel

Directed by Wes Anderson

Goldblum appears briefly as Deputy Kovacs, a lawyer with an unfortunate fate involving a cat. His three minutes of screen time are the most precisely Goldblum thing in any Wes Anderson film. That is saying something. It is also, objectively, a masterpiece.

#6 1975

Nashville

Directed by Robert Altman

Before he was famous, he was simply good. Goldblum's small role in Altman's sprawling masterpiece showed he had something rare: the ability to be interesting without saying anything at all. Altman saw it immediately. So did everyone who paid attention.

#7 1983

The Right Stuff

Directed by Philip Kaufman

Playing a NASA recruiter in Kaufman's epic about the Mercury astronaut program, Goldblum holds his own against an extraordinary cast. This period is proof that he was always going to matter — it just took the world a little while to fully catch up.

#8 2018

Isle of Dogs

Directed by Wes Anderson

Voice acting as Duke — a dog who prefaces statements with "I heard a rumor..." — Goldblum's distinctive cadence translates perfectly to animation. He and Wes Anderson are a collaboration the world deserves more of. Duke is essentially Jeff Goldblum as a dog. That's perfect.

Also Worth Watching

#9 1997

The Lost World: Jurassic Park

Directed by Steven Spielberg

Not as good as the original. Contains some of Goldblum's funniest lines. Ian Malcolm knows exactly what kind of movie he's in. He disapproves. He showed up anyway. That's the character in a sentence.

#10 1988

Earth Girls Are Easy

Directed by Julien Temple

A musical comedy about aliens (Goldblum, Jim Carrey, Damon Wayans) who crash-land in a Valley Girl's pool. It is absolutely, gloriously unhinged. Geena Davis co-stars. The hair alone justifies its existence. A genuine cult classic.

#11 1985

Into the Night

Directed by John Landis

A criminally underseen neo-noir thriller where Goldblum's insomniac everyman stumbles into danger alongside Michelle Pfeiffer. Dark, funny, and very 80s. An artifact of an era when major directors made weird, personal films and nobody stopped them.

#12 2022

Jurassic World: Dominion

Directed by Colin Trevorrow

Ian Malcolm returns, now as a philosopher-in-residence at Biosyn Genetics. The film is a mess. Jeff Goldblum is tremendous. These two facts coexist peacefully. They always have, across his entire career.
